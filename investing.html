ACTION PLAN

Your First Investor Checklist

Follow these steps to start investing like a pro.

1

Emergency Fund First

Save 3-6 months expenses in a liquid account before investing. Don't invest money you might need urgently.

2

Choose a Broker

Pick a beginner-friendly platform with low fees. Popular options: Fidelity, Vanguard, Charles Schwab.

3

Start Small

Invest your first dollar. Start with low amounts to build confidence. You can increase contributions anytime.

4

Automate & Forget

Set monthly automatic investments. Let compound interest work. Don't obsess over daily prices.

AVOID THESE

Beginner Investment Mistakes

Learn from others' mistakes before they become yours.

‚ùå

Trying to Time the Market

Even professionals can't consistently time the market. You'll miss big gains waiting for perfect entry points. Result: Miss 30% of best days = 2/3 less profit

‚úì Better: Dollar-cost average consistently
‚ùå

Panic Selling During Crashes

Markets crash ~20% every few years. Those who panic-sell lock in losses. Those who hold recover in 2-3 years. Long-term investors always win

‚úì Better: Have a plan and stick to it
‚ùå

Paying High Fees

A 2% fee vs 0.2% fee means 10x less wealth over 30 years. $10k grows to $76k vs $38k. Low-cost index funds are almost always better

‚úì Better: Choose low-fee ETFs and index funds
‚ùå

Putting All Eggs in One Stock

One company can collapse overnight. Diversification spreads risk. Owning 500 companies (via index fund) means 1 failure = 0.2% loss

‚úì Better: Build a diversified portfolio
‚ùå

Chasing Hot Tips

Your uncle's "sure thing" stock or cryptocurrency is probably a scam or already overpriced. Do your own research. Most traders underperform buy-and-hold investors

‚úì Better: Follow boring proven strategies
‚ùå

Starting Without an Emergency Fund

Emergency hits? If you invested all savings, you're forced to sell at loss. Always have 3-6 months expenses liquid first

‚úì Better: Emergency fund ‚Üí then invest
WISDOM

The Investor's Mindset

Principles that separate successful long-term investors from traders.

1

‚è∞ Time in Market

Time in market beats timing the market. Even if you invested right before a crash, staying invested recovered your losses in 5-10 years historically.

2

üéØ Diversification

Don't put all eggs in one basket. Spread across stocks, bonds, sectors, geographies. One bad investment won't destroy your portfolio.

3

üòå Emotional Control

Fear and greed are the enemies of wealth. Markets drop ~20% every few years. Panic sellers lock in losses. Patient investors capture rebounds.

4

üí∞ Dollar-Cost Averaging

Invest fixed amounts regularly (e.g., $100/month) regardless of price. Removes emotion and ensures you buy more when prices are low.
