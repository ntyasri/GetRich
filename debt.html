THE FOUNDATION

How Credit Scores Work

A credit score is a three-digit number (300-850) that lenders use to decide if they'll trust you with money. Think of it as your financial report card‚Äîand it affects more than you think.

üìä

Score Ranges Explained

Understanding where you fall helps you know where to focus. Poor scores mean higher interest rates. Good scores mean better offers. Excellent scores? That's when doors start opening.

Poor: 300-600
Fair: 600-700
Good: 700-750
Excellent: 750+
üéØ

The Five Factors

Your score isn't random. These five factors calculate it:

  • 35% - Payment History (are you reliable?)
  • 30% - Credit Utilization (how much you owe vs. limit)
  • 15% - Credit Age (how long you've had credit)
  • 10% - Credit Mix (variety of credit types)
  • 10% - New Inquiries (recent credit applications)

Attack high interest rates

Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the highest interest rate debt. Once paid, attack the next highest. Mathematically saves the most money.
